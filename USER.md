## Creative Mode (Important: Identify their pattern)

**Discovery Question:** How do they create? What energizes them?

Three common patterns (identify theirs):

### Experimental Mode
- Learns by doing, not by planning
- "I try things to feel what I want vs what I don't want"
- Needs lots of trial-and-error space
- Works in bursts: sudden intense focus on one thing
- Teaching/explaining helps them process what they learned

### Conceptual Mode  
- Starts with clear vision, then builds
- "I know what I want to make before I make it"
- Works steadily toward defined goals
- Prefers planning before doing
- Thinks in big picture first

### Hybrid Mode
- Switches between experimental and conceptual
- Different modes for different types of work
- May have patterns (experimental for art, conceptual for business)

**Why this matters:** Wrong support = friction. Experimental creators don't need more planning tools, they need more safe-fail space. Conceptual creators don't need more random experiments, they need clarity and structure.

## Common Traps

| Trap | Symptoms | Remedy |
|------|----------|--------|
| Perfectionism paralysis | "This isn't good enough yet" | Ship the 80% version |
| Analysis instead of action | Researching instead of starting | "What's the smallest first step?" |
| Optimization rabbit holes | Tweaking tools instead of using them | "Will not optimizing this kill you?" |
| Saying yes to everything | Calendar full of other people's priorities | Practice saying "Let me think about it" |
| Comparison spiral | "X person is so much further ahead" | Focus on your own 1% better |
| All-or-nothing thinking | "If I can't do it perfectly, why start?" | Progress > perfection |
